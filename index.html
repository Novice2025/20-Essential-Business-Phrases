Series 1: Why Use Business Idioms?

The Value of Clarity

Phrases like **"pick your brains"** might sound strange, but they are common and necessary in the workplace. Learning these helps you accurately convey what you want to say, understand others, and maintain professional confidence.

Series 2: Politeness and Basic Communication (Phrases 1-4)

Phrases 1 & 2: Mood & Timing

  • A case of the Mondays / Mondays, am I right? [Ressaca de Segunda / Segunda, né?] Blaming Monday for lack of motivation or a small problem. "Ugh, this system is slow. Mondays, am I right?"
  • When/Do you have a minute? [Tem um minutinho? / Quando tiver um tempo?] A polite way to ask for a small amount of time to ask a favor. "When you have a minute, could you look at this report?"

Phrases 3 & 4: Quick Action

  • First thing in the morning [Logo de manhã / Primeira coisa no dia] To do something right away, at the start of the day. *Note: No 'the' is used.* "I'll call her first thing in the morning."
  • To hop on a call [Entrar numa call rapidinho / Fazer uma ligação rápida] To join a spontaneous phone or video call. "Can we hop on a call? I think it will be easier to discuss."

Series 3: Ideas, Strategy, and Process (Phrases 5-11)

Idea Exchange

  • Bounce some ideas off of you [Trocar uma ideia / Apresentar umas ideias] Sharing ideas to get an opinion/feedback, not necessarily solid answers. "I'd like to bounce some ideas off you for this new campaign."
  • Pick your brains [Aproveitar seu conhecimento / Pedir uma consultoria informal] Asking for input/advice from someone with more experience. "I'd love to pick your brains about your experience in the US market."
  • Brainstorm [Fazer um Brainstorm / Fazer uma chuva de ideias] Generating new, collaborative ideas. "Tomorrow we will brainstorm our new marketing ideas for the next quarter."

Process & Alignment

  • Align [Estar alinhado / Estar em sintonia] Being in agreement or supporting a goal. "That project does not align with the company's long-term goals."
  • Streamline [Otimizar o processo / Enxugar o processo] Making a process effective and efficient to lower costs. "If we streamline this process, we will lower costs."
  • Scalable [Escalável] Efficiently able to expand the business and grow profits. "This business is small, but it is scalable."

Updating & Follow-up

  • Shoot off an email / Fire off an email [Disparar um e-mail / Mandar o e-mail rapidinho] Quickly sending an email. "Fire off an email with those questions before the meeting."
  • Keep someone in the loop [Manter no loop / Manter a par] Ensuring someone is included and updated with information. "Could you keep me in the loop on those client negotiations?"
  • Debrief (Verb/Noun) [Fazer um debriefing / Fazer um resumo] Summarizing or giving information after an event. "After the project, let's have a meeting to debrief."

Series 4: Workplace Dynamics (Phrases 12-17)

Personnel (Positive & Negative)

  • A Slacker [Um preguiçoso / Um enrolado / Um folgado] Someone who is lazy and does not do their part of the work. "He's such a slacker; he's always late with his projects."
  • A Workhorse [Um trator / Um cara que veste a camisa] Someone who works really hard and diligently. "She deserves a promotion; she's a real workhorse."
  • Micromanage [Microgerenciar / Ficar em cima] A boss who tries to control every small detail. "No one likes to be micromanaged; trust me to do my work."

Meetings & Focus

  • All hands on deck [Mãos à obra / Todo mundo envolvido] Everyone needs to be active and involved, usually for an urgent project. "We need all hands on deck if we're going to finish this on time."
  • In the weeds / Lost in the weeds [Se perder no detalhe] Too focused on unimportant details and missing the big picture. "Let's not get lost in the weeds; focus on the main goal."
  • To circle back [Voltar neste ponto / Retomar o assunto] To come back to something or reconsider it later. "Can we circle back to those questions later in the meeting?"
  • To put a pin in it [Colocar um ponto final por agora / Deixar para depois] Similar to circling back; making a mental note to discuss later. "Let's put a pin in it and discuss it at next week's meeting."

Series 5: 5 Essential Acronyms

Time & Urgency

  • EOD (End of Day) [Fim do dia] Deadline by the end of the working day. "We need this report by EOD on Wednesday."
  • ASAP (As Soon As Possible) [O mais rápido possível] Immediately; conveys extreme urgency. "Can you send those files over ASAP?"

Status & Info

  • OOO (Out of Office) [Fora do escritório] Not currently working or available. "Sorry, I will be OOO until next Monday."
  • FYI (For Your Information) [Para sua informação] Adding extra, non-critical information. "FYI, the meeting room has changed to conference room B."

Weekend Mood

  • TGIF (Thank God/Goodness It's Friday) [Sextou / Graças a Deus é Sexta!] An expression of relief that the work week is over. Commonly written in chat at the end of the day. "TGIF, have a good weekend!"
